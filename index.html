About

I am a mathematician working at the intersection of mathematical biology, infectious disease modeling, ecological modeling, optimal control, and data-driven decision-making. My research focuses on developing quantitative models that help understand complex biological and public-health systems.

I recently completed my Ph.D. in Mathematics at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, where my dissertation focused on modeling COVID-19 dynamics with vaccine hesitancy and developing an optimal control framework for multi-species harvesting in the southern Black Sea.

Research

My research interests include:

  • Mathematical biology and dynamical systems
  • Infectious disease modeling and vaccine hesitancy
  • Optimal control and resource management
  • Ecological and fisheries modeling
  • Data-driven modeling, forecasting, and public-health decision support
