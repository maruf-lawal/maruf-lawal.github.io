My research lies at the intersection of mathematical biology, infectious disease modeling, ecological modeling, optimal control, and data-driven decision-making.

## Infectious Disease Modeling and Vaccine Hesitancy

I develop mathematical models to study infectious disease dynamics, behavioral responses, and vaccine hesitancy. My recent work examines COVID-19 dynamics using compartmental models that incorporate hesitant and non-hesitant susceptible populations, vaccination, observed and unobserved infections, and delay-driven behavioral feedback.

## Ecological Modeling and Optimal Control

I also work on ecological and natural resource modeling, with emphasis on sustainable harvesting and ecosystem-based fisheries management. My dissertation research develops an optimal control framework for multi-species harvesting in the southern Black Sea ecosystem.

## Data-Driven Modeling and Forecasting

I am interested in combining dynamical systems, statistical methods, and machine learning tools to support forecasting and decision-making in public health and biological systems.
